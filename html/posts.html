The Start of Whatever This Is

If you have happened to stumble upon this page then congratulations! You are likely one of the few people who will ever see it. Make sure to take it in in all its glory. The beautiful CSS that I totally designed myself and did not at all use a CSS framework found at https://bulma.io. Anyway that aside this is where I will be posting things that come to mind. Nothing super interesting just things that I want to talk about to myself and possibly anyone that reads this.

You may be asking yourself why someone would even created something like this on a small website they made themselves in a few days and called their "professional portfolio"? I honestly have no idea. I am hesitent to call this a "blog" of any sort because I am sure the post frequency is going to be anything but consistent. That and blogs feel cringey despite that being exactly what I am doing with the exception of calling it something different. Either way back to the question. Again I have no idea. I guess I really enjoy talking to myself and hope that someone will enjoy listening, or maybe I really enjoy suffering having to type all of this into VSCode which was 100% NOT designed for this type of writing.

Could I have queried a content server of some sort? Probably. Could I have written this all in a Google Doc and imported it through a txt or JSON file? Yup. Could I have created my own admin panel that would allow me to make posts from a nice GUI with some grammer rules integrated through a Google API of some sort? Absolutely. Then why don't you is probably what you are thinking. Maybe I enjoy writing code in a basic text editor like some sort of vim elitest. Maybe I like making grammer mistakes everywhere thereby reinforcing the stereotype of STEM majors being completely unable to write intelligble English. Maybe I have too many personal projects that I never 100% finished, making the prospect of adding yet more work to my summer that is already packed with two research projects and a Google fellowship. Maybe I've said maybe too many times and should get off this topic.

I'm sure I'll eventually have to design some sort of system to allow writing to be a bit easier. I can only imagine how long this HTML document is going to get and I really shouldn't continue doing it this way. That probably won't stop me from making enough posts that it takes me a few extra hours to port these ones over to whatever method I come up with for long term storage. If I were smart I wouldn't let that happen, so I guess we will see.

If you happen to be a recruiter of any sort reading this your time might be better served looking at my GitHub linked in the navigation bar. Who am I to tell you what to do though? Maybe you have the same sense of humor as me. Either way I hope anyone that read this enjoyed it, or maybe felt a little more relieved to realize that none of us in CS really know what we are doing. Anyway I should go work on my glorified spreadsheet. Have a good one.
